Networked vs Stand Alone Computers
This is the subwebpage dedicated to explain diffrences between a Networked Computer and Stand Alone Computers

Stand Alone Computer: A Stand Alone Computer is a desktop or laptop that is used on its own without a connection to LAN (Local Area Network) or WAN (Wide Area Network) or a network of computers. As long as a network connection is not mandatory within a computer it is a Stand Alone Computer

Networked Computer: A Netwroked Computer is a inexpensive computer with minimal specs overall and designed to connect to the internet or a network. Instead of relying on the specs of their computer they rely on the network servers, a network can be a group of seperate computers connected together.

Stand Alone Computers
  • All your files/applications will have to be loaded onto a physical storage device. Files and data will not be accesible anywhere esle

  • If u want to print or scan a document than you need to connect the pheripherals must be connected directly

  • Damadge control is alot better as instead of infecting multiple computers with viruses only the stand alone will go bad.

  • Simplicity as you only need to manage one computer instead of a network of them

  • Conveince as all your pheripherals and anything tech related should be within arms reach and you dont have to go to a seperate location
  • New programs on stand alone computers must be set up one by one

  • Standalones are harder to monitor other users but you also do not interfere or affect other users

Networked Computers
  • Can acces files and data anywhere

  • Can monitor all networked computers activity simultaniously

  • Terrible Computer Specifications

  • Lower production costs

  • More Security Difficulties
  • Requires more of an experienced handler/manager

  • File sharing and resourse sharing is made alot easier
